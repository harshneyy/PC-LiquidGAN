Introduction

Thermal imaging has emerged as a critical sensing modality across a wide range of
applications, including medical diagnostics, surveillance, environmental monitoring, and
precision agriculture.

Unlike conventional RGB imaging, thermal imaging captures

infrared radiation emitted by objects, enabling temperature-based analysis under varying
lighting and environmental conditions.

image analysis is significantly limited by the scarcity of large-scale annotated datasets.
Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) have shown promising results in synthetic
image generation and data augmentation. By learning the underlying data distribution,
GANs can generate realistic samples to alleviate data scarcity.

Nevertheless,

conventional GAN architectures treat thermal images as generic grayscale data and fail
to incorporate the physical principles governing temperature distribution.

In reality,

thermal images inherently obey heat diffusion physics, where temperature varies
smoothly across space and follows continuity constraints derived from the heat equation.
Ignoring these physical properties may result in unrealistic temperature discontinuities,
artificial hot spots, and reduced cross-domain robustness.
To address this limitation, this work proposes a Physics-Constrained LiquidGAN (PCLiquidGAN), an extension of LiquidGAN that integrates a physics-aware loss inspired by
the steady-state heat diffusion equation. The proposed loss penalizes non-physical thermal
gradients during adversarial training, encouraging the generator to produce thermally
plausible and physically consistent images.

Literature Review

2.1

Core Papers Studied

2.1.1

LiquidGAN for Thermal Image Synthesis

Sarath and Nair [1] proposed LiquidGAN, a novel GAN architecture integrating Neural
Ordinary Differential Equations (Neural ODEs) in the generator and Liquid Neural
Networks (LNNs) in the discriminator for high-fidelity thermal image synthesis under
data scarcity.

The generator employs an encoder–ODE–decoder framework with

RK4-based latent evolution, while the LNN discriminator refines feature representations
dynamically over time.

The model demonstrates improved stability and superior

performance over DCGAN and WGAN-GP on multiple thermal datasets.
Limitation: The framework is entirely data-driven and does not explicitly enforce
physical constraints such as heat diffusion continuity within the training objective.

2.1.2

Liquid Neural Networks

Liquid Neural Networks model continuous-time neural dynamics using differential
equations [2, 3].

Their adaptive time-dependent behavior improves robustness under

distributional shifts and dynamic environments.
Limitation: Their integration in generative modeling remains limited to architectural
improvements without domain-specific physical priors.

2.1.3

Neural ODEs and Physics-Informed Learning

Neural ODEs [4] treat transformations as continuous dynamical systems, enabling smooth
latent evolution in generative models. Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) [5]
embed governing PDEs into neural training through residual minimization.

Proposed Problem Statement

Thermal image synthesis plays a crucial role in applications such as surveillance,
healthcare diagnostics, and precision agriculture, where collecting large-scale annotated
datasets is often challenging [1]. While LiquidGAN introduces continuous-time neural
dynamics for improved representation learning [1, 4], it primarily relies on data-driven
adversarial optimization without explicitly incorporating underlying thermodynamic
principles.
Thermal images inherently obey heat diffusion behavior governed by spatial continuity
and energy conservation laws.

However, existing GAN-based thermal synthesis

frameworks [6, 7], including LiquidGAN [1], treat thermal data as conventional grayscale
images and do not enforce physics-consistent constraints during training. This can result
in physically implausible temperature gradients, unrealistic heat discontinuities, and
limited cross-domain generalization under distribution shifts.
Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) [5] demonstrate that embedding governing
partial differential equations into deep learning frameworks improves physical consistency
and generalization. However, such physics-aware modeling has not been systematically
integrated into adversarial thermal image synthesis.
Furthermore, current approaches lack systematic evaluation of zero-shot transfer capability
across heterogeneous thermal domains [8], restricting their applicability in real-world lowdata scenarios.
Therefore, there is a need for a physics-informed generative framework that integrates
thermodynamically consistent constraints into adversarial training while enabling robust
zero-shot cross-domain generalization for thermally plausible image synthesis under data
scarcity.

Proposed Methodology

3.1

Overview

Building upon LiquidGAN [1],

we propose a Physics-Constrained LiquidGAN

(PC-LiquidGAN) that integrates thermodynamic principles directly into adversarial
training.
Unlike the baseline model, which treats thermal images as grayscale intensity distributions,
the proposed framework enforces consistency with the heat diffusion equation during image
synthesis.
The generator is modeled using Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (Neural ODEs) [4],
while the discriminator is implemented as a Liquid Neural Network (LNN) [2]. Physicsaware constraints are incorporated via differentiable residual losses derived from diffusion
dynamics.

Conclusion

In this work, we proposed Physics-Constrained LiquidGAN (PC-LiquidGAN) for zero-shot
cross-domain thermal image synthesis.
Unlike conventional GAN-based methods that treat thermal images as grayscale intensity
maps, our approach integrates thermodynamic constraints derived from the heat diffusion
equation.
Key contributions include:
• Neural ODE-based generator for continuous latent evolution
• Liquid Neural Network discriminator for adaptive refinement
• Flux consistency loss enforcing heat diffusion
• Energy conservation regularization
• Zero-shot cross-domain validation strategy

Experimental results demonstrate improved thermodynamic consistency, reduced physics
residual error, and enhanced robustness under domain shift.
Future work includes:
• Learning adaptive thermal diffusivity parameter α
• Extending to 3D spatio-temporal thermal modeling
• Real-time deployment for surveillance and medical systems

Future Work

Although the proposed Physics-Constrained LiquidGAN improves thermal consistency and
cross-domain robustness, several directions remain for future research.
• Learnable Diffusivity: Instead of using a fixed thermal diffusivity parameter α,
future work can explore learning this parameter directly from data to better adapt
to different thermal domains and sensor characteristics.
• Spatio-Temporal Extension: The current formulation models pseudo-temporal
evolution within the Neural ODE latent space. Extending the framework to real
thermal video sequences would allow explicit modeling of dynamic heat propagation.
• Adaptive Loss Balancing:

Automated strategies for adjusting the physics

regularization weights (µ1 , µ2 ) could improve training stability and generalization
performance.
• Multi-Physics Modeling: Incorporating additional physical processes such as
convection or radiative transfer may enhance realism in complex real-world thermal
environments.
• Real-World Deployment: Evaluating the framework under extreme domain shifts
and diverse sensor settings would further validate its practical applicability.
